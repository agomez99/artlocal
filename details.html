Artist

Jesse Trevino

Treviño’s family moved from Mexico to San Antonio, Texas, in 1948. Treviño earned an A.A. from San Antonio Junior College, a B.A. in art from Our Lady of the Lake University in San Antonio, and an M.F.A. in painting from the University of Texas at San Antonio. In 1987 he was honored with the National Hispanic Heritage Award as Artist of the Year.

Los Otros


Since the forming of the duo in 2014, Los Otros Murals has continued to use their artwork to aid local organizations in raising money to fund important missions. To date, they have raised over 40K for Child Advocates San Antonio, American Indians in Texas of the Spanish Colonial Missions, and a number of other local non-profits throughout San Antonio. Their creative ways of raising awareness and funds also spans across education and cultural staples. Los Otros has designed clothing in collaboration with New Era to bring awareness to Hispanic Heritage month. This project has been ongoing since 2017 and now benefits 27 universities across the US with half of the proceeds going back into the arts programming of each school. The duo has also helped cultural staples in San Antonio, TX like FIESTA™ San Antonio. In 2019 Los Otros were the first artists commissioned to create artwork to represent one of the largest festivals in the nation. Fiesta San Antonio is a two week long event that brings in 3.5 million visitors who’s proceeds benefit over 150 local non profits. Los Otros also plays a major part within the San Antonio public art scene as a partner of the San Antonio Street Art Initiative (SASAI). SASAI is a non-profit, 501c3, that aids in the introduction, education, and growth of public art within the street art mural genre and advocates for the careers of practicing SASAI artists.

Joey Fauerso

Joey Fauerso is an artist and Professor in the School of Art and Design at Texas State University. Her work consists mostly of painting, video, installation and performance addressing issues of gender, humor and family. Recently her work has been included in exhibitions at MASSMoCA, Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art, the Drawing Center in New York, Blue Star Contemporary in San Antonio, The David Shelton Gallery in Houston, and New Mexico State University Art Museum. Fauerso has been the recipient of multiple grants and residencies, including The Open Sessions residency at T he Drawing Center in New York, the Golden Foundation Grant, Dallas Museum of Art Kimberough Grant, the RAIR artist in residence grant, the Ucross residency grant, and recently a 3-month residency at Kunstlerhaus Bethanien in Berlin. She lives with her family in San Antonio, Texas.
